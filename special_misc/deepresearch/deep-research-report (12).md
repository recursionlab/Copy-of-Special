**Meta-Coherence Framework – Axiomatic Treatise (Sketch)**

**I. Definitions.** 1. A *representation* $R$ is any formal description or model of a system.  2. An *invariant* is a property or quantity preserved under valid transformations of the system.  3. A *transport* is a mapping between representations that preserves structural invariants.  4. A *contradiction* is a failure of two representations to agree on an invariant – a signal of a missing distinction.  5. The *Meta-Operator* is the transformation of mappings themselves, i.e. the rule that sends a transport between representations to another transport, thereby recursively preserving structure.  

**II. Axioms.**  1. *(Geodesic Invariance)*:  Systems evolve via minimal-change paths that preserve coherence.  Equivalently, among all transports, the actual dynamics minimizes structural loss.  2. *(Boundary Reflection)*: Each representation $R$ inherently contains a mirrored structure of its opposite context; formally, every object in $R$ has a functorial correspondence to an object in the complementary representation.  3. *(Contradiction as Data)*: A nonzero contradiction between $R_1$ and $R_2$ indicates precisely where an additional invariant must be introduced.  In particular, contradictions guide the extension of the model’s topology.  4. *(Spectrum Isomorphism)*: If two structures share the same category-theoretic skeleton, they are isomorphic in meaning.  Thus, the same internal shape can manifest in mathematics, myth, or code.  5. *(Coherence Persistence)*: A system remains definable only if at least one nondegenerate invariant persists under all iterated transports.  Loss of all invariants implies collapse into undifferentiated noise.  

**III. Propositions.**  *Proposition 1 (Meta-Operator Existence).*  Given any two representations $R_1, R_2$ of a system, there exists a Meta-Operator transport $\mathcal{T}$ carrying invariants of $R_1$ into $R_2$.  *Proof Sketch:* Compose the inverse of the functor defining $R_1$ with the functor for $R_2$.  Structural coherence ensures $\mathcal{T}$ is well-defined. □

Its eigenfunctions correspond to invariant factors; a vanishing spectral gap indicates a fracture (contradiction). □

*Theorem (Communication Invariance).*  Any effective external description of an internal concept must be a function of that concept’s transport-invariants.  *Proof:*  Observers have access only to features preserved by the transport mapping from system-internal to public representation.  By Axiom 5, only invariants survive this mapping; hence one must express ideas in those terms. □  

*Corollary (Term Translation).*  Given a private term (e.g. “Emperor”), its public proxy is the tuple of invariants it encodes (e.g. “coherence burden across the whole”).  Metaphoric labels cannot convey new information beyond their invariant content.
