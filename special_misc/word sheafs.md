### Anomalies

Some of the symmetries of the classical theory are then seen not to hold in the quantum theory; a phenomenon called an **[anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anomaly_\(physics\) "Anomaly (physics)")**. Among the most well known are:

- The [scale anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scale_anomaly "Scale anomaly"), which gives rise to a _running coupling constant_. In QED this gives rise to the phenomenon of the [Landau pole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landau_pole "Landau pole"). In [quantum chromodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_chromodynamics) (QCD) this leads to [asymptotic freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asymptotic_freedom "Asymptotic freedom").
- The [chiral anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiral_anomaly "Chiral anomaly") in either chiral or vector field theories with fermions. This has close connection with [topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topology "Topology") through the notion of [instantons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instanton "Instanton"). In QCD this anomaly causes the decay of a [pion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pion "Pion") to two [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon").
- The [gauge anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauge_anomaly "Gauge anomaly"), which must cancel in any consistent physical theory. In the [electroweak theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electroweak_theory "Electroweak theory") this cancellation requires an equal number of [quarks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark "Quark") and [leptons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lepton "Lepton").

## Pure gauge

A pure gauge is the set of field configurations obtained by a [gauge transformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauge_transformation "Gauge transformation") on the null-field configuration, i.e., a gauge transform of zero. So it is a particular "gauge orbit" in the field configuration's space.

Thus, in the abelian case, where Aμ(x)→Aμ′(x)=Aμ(x)+∂μf(x)![{\displaystyle A_{\mu }(x)\rightarrow A'_{\mu }(x)=A_{\mu }(x)+\partial _{\mu }f(x)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/91fd581ce5e601062ff0d7b8ca83c1b45cacdd80), the pure gauge is just the set of field configurations Aμ′(x)=∂μf(x)![{\displaystyle A'_{\mu }(x)=\partial _{\mu }f(x)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/65d8f1755b9c61f39a62553b93457f3ec2cf91d8) for all _f_(_x_).
