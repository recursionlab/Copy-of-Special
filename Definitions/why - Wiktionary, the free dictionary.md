## English

### Etymology 1

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[why](#Middle_English)*, from [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[hwȳ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hwy#Old_English "hwy")* (“why”), from [Proto-Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language "w:Proto-Germanic language") *[\*hwī](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hw%C4%AB "Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hwī")* (“”), from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*kʷey](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/k%CA%B7ey&action=edit&redlink=1 "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/kʷey (page does not exist)")*, instrumental case of *[\*kʷís](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/k%CA%B7%C3%ADs "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/kʷís")* (“who”), *[\*kʷid](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/k%CA%B7id "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/kʷid")* (“what”).

Cognate with [Old Saxon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Saxon "w:Old Saxon") *[hwī](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hwi#Old_Saxon "hwi")* (“why”), *[hwiu](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hwiu#Old_Saxon "hwiu")* (“how; why”), [Middle High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_High_German "w:Middle High German") *[wiu](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wiu#Middle_High_German "wiu")* (“how, why”), archaic [Danish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_language "w:Danish language") and [Norwegian Bokmål](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bokm%C3%A5l "w:Bokmål") *[hvi](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hvi#Danish "hvi")* (“why”), [Norwegian Nynorsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nynorsk "w:Nynorsk") *[kvi](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/kvi#Norwegian_Nynorsk "kvi")* (“why”), [Swedish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_language "w:Swedish language") *[vi](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/vi#Swedish "vi")* (“why”), [Faroese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faroese_language "w:Faroese language") and [Icelandic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icelandic_language "w:Icelandic language") *[hví](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hv%C3%AD#Faroese "hví")* (“why”), [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[quī](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/qui#Latin "qui")* (“why”), [Doric Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doric_Greek "w:Doric Greek") *[πεῖ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%80%CE%B5%E1%BF%96#Ancient_Greek "πεῖ")* (peî, “where”), [Ukrainian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukrainian_language "w:Ukrainian language") *[чи](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D1%87%D0%B8#Ukrainian "чи")* (čy, “if”), [Polish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_language "w:Polish language") *[czy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/czy#Polish "czy")*, [Czech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_language "w:Czech language") *[či](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C4%8Di#Czech "či")* (“or”), [Serbo-Croatian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbo-Croatian "w:Serbo-Croatian") *[či](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C4%8Di#Serbo-Croatian "či")* (“if”). Compare [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[þȳ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C3%BEy#Old_English "þy")* (“because, since, on that account, therefore, then”, literally “by that, for that”). #### Adverb

**why** (*not [comparable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#comparable "Appendix:Glossary")*)

1. (interrogative) For [what](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/what#English "what") [cause](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cause#English "cause"), [reason](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/reason#English "reason"), or [purpose](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/purpose#English "purpose").
	1. Introducing a complete question. quotations ▼
		***Why** is the sky blue?*
		***Why** did you do that?*
		*I don’t know **why** he did that*
		*Tell me **why** the moon changes phase.*
		1. With a negative, used rhetorically to make a suggestion.
			***Why** don't you ask her out for dinner?*
		2. Introducing a verb phrase (bare infinitive clause).
		***Why** spend money on something you already get for free?*
		***Why** not tell him how you feel?*
		3. Introducing a noun or other phrase.
		***Why** him? **Why** not someone taller?*
2. (relative) For [which](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/which#English "which") [cause](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cause#English "cause"), [reason](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/reason#English "reason"), or [purpose](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/purpose#English "purpose").
	*That's the reason **why** I did that.*
3. (fused relative) The [cause](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cause#English "cause"), [reason](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/reason#English "reason"), or [purpose](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/purpose#English "purpose") for which. quotations ▼
	*That is **why** the sky is blue.*
