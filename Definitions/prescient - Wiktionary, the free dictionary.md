## English

### Etymology

[Learned borrowing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#learned_borrowing "Appendix:Glossary") from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[praesciēns](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/praesciens#Latin "praesciens")* (“foreknowing; foretelling, predicting”), [present](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#present "Appendix:Glossary") [participle](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#participle "Appendix:Glossary") of) [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[praesciō](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/praescio#Latin "praescio")* (“to foreknow”), from *[prae-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prae-#Latin "prae-")* (*prefix meaning ‘before; in front’*) (ultimately from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*preh₂-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/preh%E2%82%82- "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/preh₂-")* (“before; in front”)) + *[sciō](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/scio#Latin "scio")* (“to know, understand; to have knowledge of”) (ultimately from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*skey-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/skey- "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/skey-")* (“to dissect; to split”)). The word is cognate with [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "w:Middle French") *[prescient](#Middle_French)* (modern [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "w:French language") *[prescient](#French)* (“prescient”)), [Italian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_language "w:Italian language") *[presciente](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/presciente#Italian "presciente")* (“prescient”).[^1] By [surface analysis](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#surface_analysis "Appendix:Glossary"), *[pre-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/pre-#English "pre-")* (“earlier in time, beforehand”) + *[scient](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/scient#English "scient")* (“knowing, aware”).

### Adjective

**prescient** (*[comparative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#comparative "Appendix:Glossary")* **more **prescient****, *[superlative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#superlative "Appendix:Glossary")* **most **prescient****)

1. [Exhibiting](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/exhibit#Verb "exhibit") or [possessing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/possess#English "possess") [prescience](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prescience#English "prescience"): having [knowledge](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/knowledge#English "knowledge") of, or [seemingly](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/seemingly#English "seemingly") able to [correctly](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/correctly#English "correctly") [predict](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/predict#English "predict"), [events](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/event#English "event") before they [take place](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/take_place#English "take place"). \[from early 17th c.\] synonyms, antonym, coordinate term ▲ quotations ▼
	Synonyms: [clairvoyant](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/clairvoyant#English "clairvoyant"), [foreknowing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/foreknowing#English "foreknowing"), [foreseeing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/foreseeing#English "foreseeing"), (obsolete) [prescious](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prescious#English "prescious"), (rare) [prescientific](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prescientific#English "prescientific"), [prevoyant](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prevoyant#English "prevoyant")
	Antonym: [unforeseeing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/unforeseeing#English "unforeseeing")
	Coordinate term: [omniscient](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/omniscient#English "omniscient")
