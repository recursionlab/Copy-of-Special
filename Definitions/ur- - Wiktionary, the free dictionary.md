## English

### Etymology

From [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "w:German language") *[ur-](#German)*, from [Middle High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_High_German "w:Middle High German") *[ur-](#Middle_High_German)*, from [Old High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_High_German "w:Old High German") *[ur-](#Old_High_German)* (“out; without”),[^1] from [Proto-West Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-West_Germanic_language "w:Proto-West Germanic language") *[\*uʀ-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-West_Germanic/u%CA%80- "Reconstruction:Proto-West Germanic/uʀ-")*, from [Proto-Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language "w:Proto-Germanic language") *[\*uz-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/uz- "Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/uz-")* (“out”), from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*uds-](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/uds-&action=edit&redlink=1 "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/uds- (page does not exist)")* (“up, out”), from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*úd](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/%C3%BAd "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/úd")* (“upwards, away, out, outwards”). [Doublet](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#doublet "Appendix:Glossary") of *[a-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/a-#English "a-")* and *[or-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/or-#English "or-")*. Cognate also with [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language "w:Dutch language") *[oer-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oer-#Dutch "oer-")* and [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language "w:Dutch language") *[oor-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oor-#Dutch "oor-")*.

### Prefix

**ur-**

1. Forming words with the sense of “ [proto-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/proto-#English "proto-"), [primitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitive#English "primitive"), [original](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/original#English "original") ”. quotations ▼

#### Usage notes

In English texts this prefix is usually (that is, more often than not) connected to the word it is attached to with a hyphen, in which case it is often also found written with a capital letter *U*. But in most cases all variants can be attested: *ur-form*, *Ur-form*, *urform*, *Urform*. However, *ur-heimat* would be unusual, since the term is borrowed from German, in which it is not hyphenated.

## Bavarian

### Etymology

From [Middle High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_High_German "w:Middle High German") *[ur-](#Middle_High_German)*, from [Old High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_High_German "w:Old High German") *[ur-](#Old_High_German)*, *[ir-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ir-#Old_High_German "ir-")* (“thoroughly”), from [Proto-Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language "w:Proto-Germanic language") *[\*uz-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/uz- "Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/uz-")* (“out”).

### Pronunciation

- [IPA](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:International_Phonetic_Alphabet "Wiktionary:International Phonetic Alphabet") <sup>(<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bavarian_phonology" title="wikipedia:Bavarian phonology">key</a>)</sup>: /uɐ̯/, /uːɐ̯/
- [Homophone](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#homophone "Appendix:Glossary"): [Uhr](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Uhr#Bavarian "Uhr")

### Prefix

**ur-**

1. [ur-](#English) ([proto-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/proto-#English "proto-"), [primitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitive#English "primitive"), [original](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/original#English "original"))
	*ur-* + *[Woid](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Woid#Bavarian "Woid")* (“forest”) → *[Urwoid](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Urwoid&action=edit&redlink=1 "Urwoid (page does not exist)")* (“primeval forest”)
	*ur-* + *[Knoi](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Knoi&action=edit&redlink=1 "Knoi (page does not exist)")* (“bang”) → *[Urknoi](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Urknoi&action=edit&redlink=1 "Urknoi (page does not exist)")* (“Big Bang”)
2. [great-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/great-#English "great-"); indicates an additional [generation](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/generation#English "generation") of separation between relatives
	*ur-* + *[Opa](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Opa#Bavarian "Opa")* (“grandfather”) → *[Uropa](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Uropa#Bavarian "Uropa")* (“great-grandfather”)
	*ur-* + *[Großtant](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Gro%C3%9Ftant&action=edit&redlink=1 "Großtant (page does not exist)")* (“great-aunt”) → *[Urgroßtant](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Urgro%C3%9Ftant&action=edit&redlink=1 "Urgroßtant (page does not exist)")* (“great-great-aunt”)
3. (chiefly East Central Bavarian, [Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vienna "w:Vienna")) [very](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/very#English "very"); used to intensify adjectives
	*ur-* + *[oid](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oid#Bavarian "oid")* (“old”) → *[uroid](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/uroid#Bavarian "uroid")* (“ancient”)
	*ur-* + *[teier](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/teier#Bavarian "teier")* (“expensive”) → *[urteier](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/urteier#Bavarian "urteier")* (“very expensive”)
	*ur-* + *[klaa](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/klaa#Bavarian "klaa")* (“small”) → *[urklaa](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/urklaa#Bavarian "urklaa")* (“very small, tiny”)
