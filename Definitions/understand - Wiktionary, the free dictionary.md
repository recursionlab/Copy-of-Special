### Etymology

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[understanden](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understanden#Middle_English "understanden")*, from [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[understandan](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understandan#Old_English "understandan")* (“to understand”), from [Proto-West Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-West_Germanic_language "w:Proto-West Germanic language") *[\*understandan](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=Reconstruction:Proto-West_Germanic/understandan&action=edit&redlink=1 "Reconstruction:Proto-West Germanic/understandan (page does not exist)")* (“to stand between, understand”), from [Proto-Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language "w:Proto-Germanic language") *[\*understandaną](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/understandan%C4%85 "Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/understandaną")* (“to stand between, understand”), equivalent to [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[under-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/under-#Old_English "under-")* (“between, inter-”) + *[standan](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/standan#Old_English "standan")* (“to stand”) (Modern English *[under-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/under-#English "under-")* + *[stand](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stand#English "stand")*). Cognate with [Old Frisian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Frisian "w:Old Frisian") *[understonda](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=understonda&action=edit&redlink=1 "understonda (page does not exist)")* (“to understand, experience, learn”), [Old High German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_High_German "w:Old High German") *[understantan](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=understantan&action=edit&redlink=1 "understantan (page does not exist)")* (“to understand”), Middle [Danish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_language "w:Danish language") *[understande](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understande#Danish "understande")* (“to understand”). Compare also [Saterland Frisian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saterland_Frisian_language "w:Saterland Frisian language") *[understunda](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=understunda&action=edit&redlink=1 "understunda (page does not exist)")*, *[unnerstounde](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=unnerstounde&action=edit&redlink=1 "unnerstounde (page does not exist)")* (“to dare, survey, measure”), [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language "w:Dutch language") *[onderstaan](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=onderstaan&action=edit&redlink=1 "onderstaan (page does not exist)")* (“to undertake, presume”), [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "w:German language") *[unterstehen](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/unterstehen#German "unterstehen")* (“to be subordinate”).

### Verb

**understand** (*third-person singular simple present* **[understands](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understands#English "understands")**, *present participle* **[understanding](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understanding#English "understanding")**, *simple past and past participle* **[understood](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understood#English "understood")**)

1. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), [intransitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#intransitive "Appendix:Glossary"), of [communication](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/communication#English "communication") or means of communication: words, statements, signs, etc.) To [know](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/know#English "know") the [meaning](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/meaning#English "meaning") of; to [parse](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/parse#English "parse") or have parsed correctly; to [comprehend](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/comprehend#English "comprehend"). quotations ▼
	*Can you repeat what you just said? I didn't **understand**.*
	1. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), generally) To know the meaning of. quotations ▼
2. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), of a skill, task, profession, etc.) To be thoroughly familiar with; to be able to [undertake](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/undertake#English "undertake") [properly](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/properly#English "properly").
	*The students **understood** the assignment.*
3. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), usually with clause as object) To [comprehend](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/comprehend#English "comprehend") a [fact](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fact#English "fact") or [principle](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/principle#English "principle"); to regard or come to regard a [belief](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/belief#English "belief") as such. quotations ▼
	*I **understand** that company policy says I can't get a refund, but can you make an exception?*
	*There's been no formal declaration, but it's **understood** that a state of war exists between the two countries*
4. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), of people) To [know](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/know#English "know") the [intent](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/intent#English "intent"), [motives](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/motives#English "motives") or [character](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/character#English "character") of; (of events) to know the causes of or reasons for.
	*One day you say you love me, the next you ignore me—I don't **understand** you!*
5. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary")) To [believe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/believe#English "believe") or [infer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/infer#English "infer"), to [think](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/think#English "think") one grasps [sufficiently](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sufficiently#English "sufficiently") despite potentially incomplete [knowledge](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/knowledge#English "knowledge").
	*I've been given to **understand** that you have a package for me?*
	*At least four people are **understood** to have been injured.*
6. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), [grammar](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/grammar#English "grammar")) To [regard](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/regard#English "regard") as present when not. quotations ▼
	*In the imperative mood, the word “you” is usually **understood**.*
7. ([humorous](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#humorous "Appendix:Glossary"), [rare](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#rare "Appendix:Glossary"), [obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary") outside circus, acrobatics) To [stand](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stand#English "stand") [underneath](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/underneath#English "underneath"), to [support](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/support#English "support"). quotations ▼

#### Usage notes

- In its sense of "imputing meaning", use is usually limited to the past participle *understood*.
- The obsolete perfect form *[understanded](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understanded "understanded")* is occasionally found, e.g. in the *[Book of Common Prayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Common_Prayer "w:Book of Common Prayer")* and the *[39 Articles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/39_Articles "w:39 Articles") of the Anglican Church*.
