## English

### Etymology

[Learned borrowing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#learned_borrowing "Appendix:Glossary") from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") (“a putting together”). By [surface analysis](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#surface_analysis "Appendix:Glossary"), *[col-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/col-#English "col-")* (“together”) + *[location](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/location#English "location")*. The technical sense in linguistics was established in 1951, although it may actually be earlier. First attested in 1605.

**collocation** (*[countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary") and [uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")*, *plural* **[collocations](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/collocations#English "collocations")**)

1. ([uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")) The [grouping](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/grouping#English "grouping") or [juxtaposition](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/juxtaposition#English "juxtaposition") of things, especially [words](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/words#English "words") or [sounds](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sounds#English "sounds"). quotations ▼
2. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary")) Such a specific grouping. quotations ▼
3. ([linguistics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/linguistics#English "linguistics"), [translation studies](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/translation_studies#English "translation studies")) A sequence of words or terms that co-occur more often than would be expected by chance (i.e., the [statistically](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/statistically#English "statistically") [significant](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/significant#English "significant") placement of particular words in a [language](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/language#English "language")), often representing an established name for, or idiomatic way of conveying, a particular semantic concept. hyponyms ▼ quotations ▼
4. ([mathematics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mathematics#English "mathematics")) A method of finding an approximate solution of an ordinary differential equation ${\displaystyle L[y]=0}$ by determining coefficients in an expansion ${\textstyle y(x)=y_{0}(x)+\sum _{l=0}^{q}\alpha _{l}y_{l}(x)}$ so as to make ${\displaystyle L[y]}$ vanish at prescribed points; the expansion with the coefficients thus found is the sought approximation.
5. ([computing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/computing#Noun "computing")) A service allowing multiple [customers](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/customer#English "customer") to locate [network](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/network#English "network"), [server](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/server#English "server"), and storage gear and connect them to a variety of [telecommunications](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/telecommunication#English "telecommunication") and network service providers, at a minimum of cost and complexity. quotations ▼
