This is a glossary of terms and definitions related to the topic of [set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_theory "Set theory").

## Greek

α

Often used for an [ordinal](#ordinal)

β

1\. βX is the [Stone–Čech compactification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone%E2%80%93%C4%8Cech_compactification "Stone–Čech compactification") of X

2\. An ordinal

γ

A [gamma number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Additively_indecomposable_ordinal "Additively indecomposable ordinal"), an [ordinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinal_number "Ordinal number") of the form ω <sup>α</sup>

Γ

The [Gamma function of ordinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veblen_function "Veblen function"). In particular Γ <sub>0</sub> is the [Feferman–Schütte ordinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feferman%E2%80%93Sch%C3%BCtte_ordinal "Feferman–Schütte ordinal").

δ

1\. A [delta number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_number "Delta number") is an ordinal of the form ω <sup>ω <sup>α</sup></sup>

2\. A limit ordinal

Δ (Greek capital delta, not to be confused with a triangle ∆)

1\. A set of formulas in the [Lévy hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9vy_hierarchy "Lévy hierarchy")

2\. A [delta system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_system "Delta system")

ε

An [epsilon number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epsilon_numbers_\(mathematics\) "Epsilon numbers (mathematics)"), an ordinal with *ω <sup>ε</sup>* = *ε*

η

1\. The [order type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_type "Order type") of the [rational numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rational_number "Rational number")

2\. An [eta set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eta_set "Eta set"), a type of ordered set

3\. η <sub>α</sub> is an [Erdős cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erd%C5%91s_cardinal "Erdős cardinal")

θ

The [order type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_type "Order type") of the [real numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number "Real number")

Θ

The supremum of the ordinals that are the image of a function from <sup>ω</sup> ω (usually in models where the axiom of choice is not assumed)

κ

1\. Often used for a [cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_number "Cardinal number"), especially the critical point of an elementary embedding

2\. The [Erdős cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erd%C5%91s_cardinal "Erdős cardinal") *κ* (*α*) is the smallest cardinal such that *κ* (*α*) → (*α*) <sup>&lt; <i>ω</i></sup>

λ

1\. Often used for a [cardinal](#cardinal)

2\. The order type of the [real numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number "Real number")

μ

A [measure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measure_\(mathematics\) "Measure (mathematics)")

Π

1\. A product of cardinals

2\. A set of formulas in the [Lévy hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9vy_hierarchy "Lévy hierarchy")

ρ

The [rank of a set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rank_of_a_set "Rank of a set")

σ

countable, as in [σ-compact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%A3-compact "Σ-compact"), σ-complete and so on

Σ

1\. A sum of cardinals

2\. A set of formulas in the [Lévy hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9vy_hierarchy "Lévy hierarchy")

φ

A [Veblen function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veblen_function "Veblen function")

ω

1\. The smallest infinite ordinal

2\. ω <sub>α</sub> is an alternative name for ℵ <sub><i>α</i></sub>, used when it is considered as an ordinal number rather than a cardinal number

Ω

1\. The class of all ordinals, related to [Cantor's absolute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantor%27s_absolute "Cantor's absolute")

2\. [Ω-logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%A9-logic "Ω-logic") is a form of logic introduced by Hugh Woodin

An equivalence relation

⨡

*f* ⨡ *X* is now the restriction of a function or relation *f* to some set *X*, though its original meaning was the corestriction

↿

*f* ↿ *X* is the restriction of a function or relation *f* to some set *X*

∆ (A triangle, not to be confused with the Greek letter Δ)

1\. The symmetric difference of two sets

2\. A [diagonal intersection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diagonal_intersection "Diagonal intersection")

The [composition of functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Function_composition "Function composition")

⁀

*s* ⁀ *x* is the extension of a sequence *s* by *x*

+

1\. [Addition of ordinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinal_arithmetic#Addition "Ordinal arithmetic")

2\. [Addition of cardinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_number#Cardinal_addition "Cardinal number")

3\. *α* <sup>+</sup> is the smallest cardinal greater than *α*

4\. *B* <sup>+</sup> is the poset of nonzero elements of a Boolean algebra *B*

5\. The inclusive or operation in a Boolean algebra. (In ring theory it is used for the exclusive or operation)

~

1\. The difference of two sets: *x* ~ *y* is the set of elements of *x* not in *y*.

2\. An equivalence relation

∖

The difference of two sets: *x* ∖ *y* is the set of elements of *x* not in *y*.

−

The difference of two sets: *x* − *y* is the set of elements of *x* not in *y*.

≈

Has the [same cardinality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equinumerosity "Equinumerosity") as

×

A [product of sets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Product_of_sets "Product of sets")

/

A quotient of a set by an [equivalence relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equivalence_relation "Equivalence relation")

⋅

1\. *x* ⋅ *y* is the ordinal product of two ordinals

2\. *x* ⋅ *y* is the cardinal product of two cardinals

\*

An operation that takes a forcing poset and a name for a forcing poset and produces a new forcing poset.

∞

The class of all ordinals, or at least something larger than all ordinals

${\displaystyle \alpha ^{\beta }}$

1\. Cardinal exponentiation

2\. Ordinal exponentiation

${\displaystyle {}^{\beta }\alpha }$

1\. The set of functions from *β* to *α*

→

1\. Implies

2\. *f*: *X* → *Y* means *f* is a function from *X* to *Y*.

3\. The [ordinary partition symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinary_partition_symbol "Ordinary partition symbol"), where *κ* →(*λ*) <sup><i>n</i></sup>  
<sub><i>m</i></sub> means that for every coloring of the *n* -element subsets of κ with *m* colors there is a subset of size λ all of whose *n* -element subsets are the same color.

*f* ′ *x*

If there is a unique *y* such that ⟨ *x*,*y* ⟩ is in *f* then *f* ′ *x* is *y*, otherwise it is the empty set. So if *f* is a function and *x* is in its domain, then *f* ′ *x* is *f* (*x*).

*f* ″ *X*

*f* ″ *X* is the image of a set *X* by *f*. If *f* is a function whose domain contains *X* this is { *f* (*x*):*x* ∈ *X* }

\[ \]

1\. *M* \[*G*\] is the smallest model of ZF containing *G* and all elements of *M*.

2\. \[*α*\] <sup><i>β</i></sup> is the set of all subsets of a set *α* of cardinality *β*, or of an ordered set *α* of order type *β*

3\. \[*x*\] is the equivalence class of *x*

{ }

1\. { *a*, *b*,...} is the set with elements *a*, *b*,...

2\. { *x*: *φ* (*x*)} is the set of *x* such that *φ* (*x*)

⟨ ⟩

⟨ *a*,*b* ⟩ is an [ordered pair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordered_pair "Ordered pair"), and similarly for ordered *n* -tuples

${\displaystyle |X|}$

The cardinality of a set *X*

${\displaystyle \|\varphi \|}$

The value of a formula *φ* in some Boolean algebra

⌜ *φ* ⌝

⌜ *φ* ⌝ ([Quine quotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quine_quotes "Quine quotes"), unicode U+231C, U+231D) is the [Gödel number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6del_number "Gödel number") of a formula *φ*

⊦

*A* ⊦ *φ* means that the formula *φ* follows from the theory *A*

⊧

*A* ⊧ *φ* means that the formula *φ* holds in the [model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_theory "Model theory") *A*

⊩

The [forcing relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forcing_relation "Forcing relation")

≺

An [elementary embedding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_embedding "Elementary embedding")

⊥

The [false](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/False_\(logic\) "False (logic)") symbol

*p* ⊥ *q* means that *p* and *q* are incompatible elements of a partial order

0 <sup>#</sup>

[zero sharp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero_sharp "Zero sharp"), the set of true formulas about indiscernibles and order-indiscernibles in the constructible universe

0 <sup>†</sup>

[zero dagger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero_dagger "Zero dagger"), a certain set of true formulas

⁠ ${\displaystyle \aleph }$ ⁠

The Hebrew letter [aleph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleph "Aleph"), which indexes the [aleph numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleph_number "Aleph number") or infinite cardinals ℵ <sub><i>α</i></sub>

⁠ ${\displaystyle \beth }$ ⁠

The Hebrew letter [beth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beth_\(letter\) "Beth (letter)"), which indexes the [beth numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beth_number "Beth number") ב <sub><i>α</i></sub>

${\displaystyle \gimel }$

A serif form of the Hebrew letter [gimel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gimel "Gimel"), representing the [gimel function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gimel_function "Gimel function") ${\displaystyle \gimel (\kappa )=\kappa ^{\operatorname {cf} \kappa }}$

ת

## A

𝔞

The almost disjointness number, the least size of a maximal [almost disjoint family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almost_disjoint_family "Almost disjoint family") of infinite subsets of *ω*

A

The [Suslin operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suslin_operation "Suslin operation")

absolute

1\. A statement is called [absolute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absoluteness_\(logic\) "Absoluteness (logic)") if its truth in some model implies its truth in certain related models

2\. Cantor's absolute is a somewhat unclear concept sometimes used to mean the class of all sets

3\. Cantor's [Absolute infinite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_infinite "Absolute infinite") Ω is a somewhat unclear concept related to the class of all ordinals

AC

1\. AC is the [Axiom of choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_choice "Axiom of choice")

2\. AC <sub><i>ω</i></sub> is the [Axiom of countable choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_countable_choice "Axiom of countable choice")

AD

The [axiom of determinacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_determinacy "Axiom of determinacy")

add

additivity

The additivity add(*I*) of I is the smallest number of sets of I with union not in I

additively

An ordinal is called [additively indecomposable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Additively_indecomposable "Additively indecomposable") if it is not the sum of a finite number of smaller ordinals. These are the same as [gamma numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Additively_indecomposable_ordinal "Additively indecomposable ordinal") or powers of *ω*.

admissible

An [admissible set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Admissible_set "Admissible set") is a model of Kripke–Platek set theory, and an [admissible ordinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Admissible_ordinal "Admissible ordinal") is an ordinal α such that *L* <sub><i>α</i></sub> is an admissible set

AH

The [generalized continuum hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generalized_continuum_hypothesis "Generalized continuum hypothesis") states that 2 <sup>ℵ <sub><i>α</i></sub></sup> = ℵ <sub><i>α</i> +1</sub>

aleph

1\. The Hebrew letter [ℵ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleph "Aleph")

2\. An infinite cardinal

3\. The [aleph function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleph_function "Aleph function") taking ordinals to infinite cardinals

4\. The [aleph hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleph_hypothesis "Aleph hypothesis") is a form of the generalized continuum hypothesis

almost universal

A class is called [almost universal](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Almost_universal&action=edit&redlink=1 "Almost universal (page does not exist)") if every subset of it is contained in some member of it

amenable

An [amenable set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amenable_set "Amenable set") is a set that is a model of Kripke–Platek set theory without the axiom of collection

analytic

An [analytic set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_set "Analytic set") is the continuous image of a Polish space. (This is not the same as an analytical set)

analytical

The [analytical hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytical_hierarchy "Analytical hierarchy") is a hierarchy of subsets of an effective Polish space (such as *ω*). They are definable by a second-order formula without parameters, and an analytical set is a set in the analytical hierarchy. (This is not the same as an analytic set)

antichain

An [antichain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antichain "Antichain") is a set of pairwise incompatible elements of a poset

[anti-foundation axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aczel%27s_anti-foundation_axiom "Aczel's anti-foundation axiom")

An axiom in set theory that allows for the existence of non-well-founded sets, in contrast to the traditional [foundation axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_regularity "Axiom of regularity") which prohibits such sets.

antinomy

paradox

arithmetic

The [ordinal arithmetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinal_arithmetic "Ordinal arithmetic") is arithmetic on [ordinal numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinal_number "Ordinal number")

The [cardinal arithmetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_arithmetic "Cardinal arithmetic") is arithmetic on [cardinal numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_number "Cardinal number")

arithmetical

The [arithmetical hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arithmetical_hierarchy "Arithmetical hierarchy") is a hierarchy of subsets of a Polish space that can be defined by first-order formulas

Aronszajn

1\. [Nachman Aronszajn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nachman_Aronszajn "Nachman Aronszajn")

2\. An [Aronszajn tree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronszajn_tree "Aronszajn tree") is an uncountable tree such that all branches and levels are countable. More generally a *κ* - [Aronszajn tree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aronszajn_tree "Aronszajn tree") is a tree of cardinality *κ* such that all branches and levels have cardinality less than *κ*

atom

1\. An [urelement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urelement "Urelement"), something that is not a set but allowed to be an element of a set

2\. An element of a poset such that any two elements smaller than it are compatible.

3\. A set of positive measure such that every measurable subset has the same measure or measure 0

atomic

An atomic formula (in set theory) is one of the form *x* = *y* or *x* ∈ *y*

axiom

[Aczel's anti-foundation axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aczel%27s_anti-foundation_axiom "Aczel's anti-foundation axiom") states that every accessible pointed directed graph corresponds to a unique set

[AD+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AD%2B "AD+") An extension of the axiom of determinacy

[Axiom F](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_F "Axiom F") states that the class of all ordinals is Mahlo

[Axiom of adjunction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_adjunction "Axiom of adjunction") Adjoining a set to another set produces a set

[Axiom of amalgamation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_amalgamation "Axiom of amalgamation") The union of all elements of a set is a set. Same as axiom of union

[Axiom of choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_choice "Axiom of choice") The product of any set of non-empty sets is non-empty

[Axiom of collection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_collection "Axiom of collection") This can mean either the axiom of replacement or the axiom of separation

[Axiom of comprehension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_comprehension "Axiom of comprehension") The class of all sets with a given property is a set. Usually contradictory.

[Axiom of constructibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_constructibility "Axiom of constructibility") Any set is constructible, often abbreviated as *V* = *L*

[Axiom of countability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_countability "Axiom of countability") Every set is hereditarily countable

[Axiom of countable choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_countable_choice "Axiom of countable choice") The product of a countable number of non-empty sets is non-empty

[Axiom of dependent choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_dependent_choice "Axiom of dependent choice") A weak form of the axiom of choice

[Axiom of determinacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_determinacy "Axiom of determinacy") Certain games are determined, in other words one player has a winning strategy

[Axiom of elementary sets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_elementary_sets "Axiom of elementary sets") describes the sets with 0, 1, or 2 elements

[Axiom of empty set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_empty_set "Axiom of empty set") The empty set exists

[Axiom of extensionality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_extensionality "Axiom of extensionality") or axiom of extent

[Axiom of finite choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_finite_choice "Axiom of finite choice") Any product of non-empty finite sets is non-empty

[Axiom of foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_foundation "Axiom of foundation") Same as axiom of regularity

[Axiom of global choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_global_choice "Axiom of global choice") There is a global choice function

[Axiom of heredity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_heredity "Axiom of heredity") (any member of a set is a set; used in Ackermann's system.)

[Axiom of infinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_infinity "Axiom of infinity") There is an infinite set

[Axiom of limitation of size](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_limitation_of_size "Axiom of limitation of size") A class is a set if and only if it has smaller cardinality than the class of all sets

[Axiom of pairing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_pairing "Axiom of pairing") Unordered pairs of sets are sets

[Axiom of power set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_power_set "Axiom of power set") The powerset of any set is a set

[Axiom of projective determinacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_projective_determinacy "Axiom of projective determinacy") Certain games given by projective set are determined, in other words one player has a winning strategy

[Axiom of real determinacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_real_determinacy "Axiom of real determinacy") Certain games are determined, in other words one player has a winning strategy

[Axiom of regularity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_regularity "Axiom of regularity") Sets are well founded

[Axiom of replacement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_replacement "Axiom of replacement") The image of a set under a function is a set. Same as axiom of substitution

[Axiom of subsets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_subsets "Axiom of subsets") The powerset of a set is a set. Same as axiom of powersets

[Axiom of substitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_substitution "Axiom of substitution") The image of a set under a function is a set

[Axiom of union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_union "Axiom of union") The union of all elements of a set is a set

[Axiom schema of predicative separation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_schema_of_predicative_separation "Axiom schema of predicative separation") Axiom of separation for formulas whose quantifiers are bounded

[Axiom schema of replacement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_schema_of_replacement "Axiom schema of replacement") The image of a set under a function is a set

[Axiom schema of separation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_schema_of_separation "Axiom schema of separation") The elements of a set with some property form a set

[Axiom schema of specification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_schema_of_specification "Axiom schema of specification") The elements of a set with some property form a set. Same as axiom schema of separation

[Freiling's axiom of symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freiling%27s_axiom_of_symmetry "Freiling's axiom of symmetry") is equivalent to the negation of the continuum hypothesis

[Martin's axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin%27s_axiom "Martin's axiom") states very roughly that cardinals less than the cardinality of the continuum behave like ℵ <sub>0</sub>.

The [proper forcing axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proper_forcing_axiom "Proper forcing axiom") is a strengthening of Martin's axiom

## B

𝔟

The [bounding number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bounding_number "Bounding number"), the least size of an unbounded family of sequences of natural numbers

B

A [Boolean algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boolean_algebra "Boolean algebra")

BA

[Baumgartner's axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baumgartner%27s_axiom "Baumgartner's axiom"), one of three axioms introduced by Baumgartner.

BACH

[Baumgartner's axiom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baumgartner%27s_axiom "Baumgartner's axiom") plus the continuum hypothesis.

Baire

1\. [René-Louis Baire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9-Louis_Baire "René-Louis Baire")

2\. A subset of a topological space has the [Baire property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baire_property "Baire property") if it differs from an open set by a meager set

3\. The [Baire space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baire_space_\(set_theory\) "Baire space (set theory)") is a topological space whose points are sequences of natural numbers

4\. A [Baire space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baire_space "Baire space") is a topological space such that every intersection of a countable collection of open dense sets is dense

basic set theory

1\. [Naive set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naive_set_theory "Naive set theory")

2\. A weak set theory, given by [Kripke–Platek set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kripke%E2%80%93Platek_set_theory "Kripke–Platek set theory") without the axiom of collection. Sometimes also called "rudimentary set theory".[^1]

BC

[Berkeley cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_cardinal "Berkeley cardinal")

BD

[Borel determinacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borel_determinacy "Borel determinacy")

Berkeley cardinal

A [Berkeley cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_cardinal "Berkeley cardinal") is a cardinal κ in a model of ZF such that for every transitive set *M* that includes κ, there is a nontrivial elementary embedding of *M* into *M* with critical point below κ.

Bernays

1\. [Paul Bernays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Bernays "Paul Bernays")

2\. [Bernays–Gödel set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernays%E2%80%93G%C3%B6del_set_theory "Bernays–Gödel set theory") is a set theory with classes

Berry's paradox

[Berry's paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berry%27s_paradox "Berry's paradox") considers the smallest positive integer not definable in ten words

beth

1\. The Hebrew letter [ב](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beth_\(letter\) "Beth (letter)")

2\. A [beth number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beth_number "Beth number") ב <sub>α</sub>

Beth

[Evert Willem Beth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evert_Willem_Beth "Evert Willem Beth"), as in [Beth definability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beth_definability "Beth definability")

BG

## W

weakly

1\. A [weakly inaccessible cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weakly_inaccessible_cardinal "Weakly inaccessible cardinal") is a regular weak limit cardinal

2\. A [weakly compact cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weakly_compact_cardinal "Weakly compact cardinal") is a cardinal κ (usually also assumed to be inaccessible) such that the infinitary language L <sub>κ,κ</sub> satisfies the weak compactness theorem

3\. A [weakly Mahlo cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weakly_Mahlo_cardinal "Weakly Mahlo cardinal") is a cardinal κ that is weakly inaccessible and such that the set of weakly inaccessible cardinals less than κ is stationary in κ

well-founded

A relation is called [well-founded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-founded "Well-founded") if every non-empty subset has a minimal element (otherwise it is "non-well-founded")

well-ordering

A [well-ordering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-ordering "Well-ordering") is a well founded relation, usually also assumed to be a total order

[well-ordering principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-ordering_principle "Well-ordering principle")

that the positive integers are well-ordered, i.e., every non-empty set of positive integers contains a least element

[well-ordering theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-ordering_theorem "Well-ordering theorem")

that every set can be well-ordered

Wf

The class of well-founded sets, which is the same as the class of all sets if one assumes the axiom of foundation

Woodin

1\. [Hugh Woodin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugh_Woodin "Hugh Woodin")

2\. A [Woodin cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodin_cardinal "Woodin cardinal") is a type of large cardinal that is the critical point of a certain sort of elementary embedding, closely related to the axiom of projective determinacy
