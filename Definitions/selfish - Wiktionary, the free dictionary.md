## English

### Alternative forms

- [selvish](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selvish#English "selvish") ([obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary"))

### Etymology

From + *[\-ish](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-ish#English "-ish")*. Compare [Danish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danish_language "w:Danish language") *[selvisk](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selvisk#Danish "selvisk")* (“selfish”), [Swedish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_language "w:Swedish language") *[självisk](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sj%C3%A4lvisk#Swedish "självisk")* (“selfish”).

### Adjective

**selfish** (*[comparative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#comparative "Appendix:Glossary")* **more **selfish**** *or* ([proscribed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#proscribed "Appendix:Glossary")) **[selfisher](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selfisher#English "selfisher")**, *[superlative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#superlative "Appendix:Glossary")* **most **selfish**** *or* ([proscribed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#proscribed "Appendix:Glossary")) **[selfishest](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selfishest#English "selfishest")**)

1. (clarification of this definition is needed)(The addition of [quotations](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:QUOTE "Wiktionary:QUOTE") indicative of this usage is being sought:) Holding one's own [self-interest](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/self-interest#English "self-interest") as the standard for [decision making](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/decision_making#English "decision making"). quotations ▼
2. Having excessive or exclusive regard and concern for one's own well-being, profit or pleasure, with little or no consideration for others. synonyms, antonyms ▲ quotations ▼
	Synonyms: [egoistic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/egoistic#English "egoistic"), [egotistic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/egotistic#English "egotistic"), [egotistical](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/egotistical#English "egotistical"), [self-centered](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/self-centered#English "self-centered"), [greedy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/greedy#English "greedy"); *see also* [Thesaurus:selfish](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Thesaurus:selfish#English "Thesaurus:selfish")
	Antonyms: [altruistic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/altruistic#English "altruistic"), [otherish](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/otherish#English "otherish"), [philanthropic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philanthropic#English "philanthropic"), [selfless](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selfless#English "selfless"), [unselfish](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/unselfish#English "unselfish"); *see also* [Thesaurus:self-abnegatory](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Thesaurus:self-abnegatory#English "Thesaurus:self-abnegatory")
3. ([video games](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/video_game#English "video game")) Of video game [characters](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/character#Noun "character"): relying on their own actions and capabilities to be effective in the game, rather than on other characters. quotations ▼
4. ([biology](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/biology#English "biology")) Related to or equivalent to a [selfish gene](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/selfish_gene#English "selfish gene"); best understood as existing to preserve and replicate itself in competition with other entities at the same level of existence, rather than at the level of the individual or population containing it. quotations ▼

#### Usage notes

- Said of people and their thoughts and actions, such as motives, desires, acts.
