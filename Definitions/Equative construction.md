An **equative construction** is a [grammatical construction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammatical_construction "Grammatical construction") using an [adjective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjective "Adjective") or an [adverb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adverb "Adverb") in the [comparative of equality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparative_of_equality "Comparative of equality") (also called "equative degree"). An equative construction equates a comparee with a standard in terms of a certain quality or action. In [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language "English language"), such a construction takes the form “as X as S”, where S is the standard.[^1]

In [modern French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_French "Modern French"), the equative construction takes the form “aussi X que S”.[^2]

[Welsh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_language "Welsh language") has an equative degree of adjectives, usually indicated by the suffix *\-ed*; for example, " *hyned* " (*â*...)", meaning "as old (as...)".[^3]
