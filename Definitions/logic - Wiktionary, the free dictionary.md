### Etymology 1

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[logike](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logike#Middle_English "logike")*, from [Old French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French "w:Old French") and [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[logicus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logicus#Latin "logicus")*, from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[λογῐκός](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BB%CE%BF%CE%B3%CE%B9%CE%BA%CF%8C%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "λογικός")* (logĭkós).

### Etymology 2

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[logik](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logik#Middle_English "logik")*, from [Old French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French "w:Old French") *[logike](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logike#Old_French "logike")*, from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[logica](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logica#Latin "logica")*, from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[λογική](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BB%CE%BF%CE%B3%CE%B9%CE%BA%CE%AE#Ancient_Greek "λογική")* (logikḗ, “logic”), from feminine of *[λογικός](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BB%CE%BF%CE%B3%CE%B9%CE%BA%CF%8C%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "λογικός")* (logikós, “of or pertaining to speech or reason or reasoning, rational, reasonable”), from *[λόγος](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BB%CF%8C%CE%B3%CE%BF%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "λόγος")* (lógos, “speech, reason”). Displaced native [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[flitcræft](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/flitcr%C3%A6ft#Old_English "flitcræft")* (literally “art of arguing”).

#### Noun

**logic** (*[countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary") and [uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")*, *plural* **[logics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logics#English "logics")**)

1. ([uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")) A method of human [thought](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/thought#English "thought") that involves thinking in a [linear](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/linear#English "linear"), step-by-step manner about how a problem can be solved. Logic is the basis of many principles including the [scientific method](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/scientific_method#English "scientific method").
2. ([philosophy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philosophy#English "philosophy"), logic) The study of the principles and criteria of valid [inference](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/inference#English "inference") and [demonstration](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/demonstration#English "demonstration"). hyponym ▼ quotations ▼
3. ([uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary"), [mathematics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mathematics#English "mathematics")) The mathematical study of relationships between rigorously [defined](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/define#English "define") concepts and of mathematical [proof](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/proof#English "proof") of [statements](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/statement#English "statement").
4. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary"), [mathematics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mathematics#English "mathematics")) A [formal](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/formal#English "formal") or [informal](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/informal#English "informal") [language](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/language#English "language") together with a [deductive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/deductive#English "deductive") [system](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/system#English "system") or a [model-theoretic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/model_theory#English "model theory") [semantics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/semantics#English "semantics").
5. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary")) Any [system](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/system#English "system") of thought, whether [rigorous](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rigorous#English "rigorous") and [productive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/productive#English "productive") or not, especially one associated with a particular person. quotations ▼
	*It's hard to work out his system of **logic**.*
6. ([uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")) The part of a system (usually electronic) that performs the boolean logic operations, short for [logic gates](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logic_gate#English "logic gate") or [logic circuit](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logic_circuit#English "logic circuit").
	*Fred is designing the **logic** for the new controller.*
7. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary"), [sociology](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sociology#English "sociology")) A [system](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/system#English "system") of thought or collection of rhetoric, especially one associated with a social practice. quotations ▼
