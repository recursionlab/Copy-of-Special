## English

### Etymology

From [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[χαρακτηριστικός](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%87%CE%B1%CF%81%CE%B1%CE%BA%CF%84%CE%B7%CF%81%CE%B9%CF%83%CF%84%CE%B9%CE%BA%CF%8C%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "χαρακτηριστικός")* (kharaktēristikós), from *[χαρακτηρίζω](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%87%CE%B1%CF%81%CE%B1%CE%BA%CF%84%CE%B7%CF%81%CE%AF%CE%B6%CF%89#Ancient_Greek "χαρακτηρίζω")* (kharaktērízō, “to designate by a characteristic mark”), from *[χαρακτήρ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%87%CE%B1%CF%81%CE%B1%CE%BA%CF%84%CE%AE%CF%81#Ancient_Greek "χαρακτήρ")* (kharaktḗr, “a mark, character”). By [surface analysis](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#surface_analysis "Appendix:Glossary"), + *[\-istic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-istic#English "-istic")*.

### Noun

**characteristic** (*plural* **[characteristics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/characteristics#English "characteristics")**)

1. A [distinguishing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/distinguishing#English "distinguishing") [feature](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/feature#English "feature") of a person or thing, a part of mental or physical behavior.
2. ([mathematics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mathematics#English "mathematics")) The [integer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/integer#English "integer") part of a [logarithm](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logarithm#English "logarithm"). quotations ▼
3. ([nautical](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/nautical#English "nautical")) The distinguishing features of a [navigational](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/navigational#English "navigational") [light](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/light#English "light") on a [lighthouse](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/lighthouse#English "lighthouse") etc by which it can be identified (colour, pattern of flashes etc.).
4. ([algebra](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/algebra#English "algebra"), field theory, ring theory) For a given [field](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/field#English "field") or [ring](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ring#English "ring"), a [natural number](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/natural_number#English "natural number") that is either the smallest positive number *n* such that *n* instances of the [multiplicative identity](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/multiplicative_identity#English "multiplicative identity") (1) summed together yield the [additive identity](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/additive_identity#English "additive identity") (0) or, if no such number exists, the number 0. quotations ▼
	*The **characteristic** of a field, if non-zero, must be a prime number.*
