### Etymology

The noun is derived from [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[stile](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stile#Middle_English "stile")*, *[stel](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stel#Middle_English "stel")*, *[stele](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stele#Middle_English "stele")*, *[stiel](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stiel#Middle_English "stiel")*, *[stiele](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stiele#Middle_English "stiele")*, *[stil](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stil#Middle_English "stil")*, *[still](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/still#Middle_English "still")*, *[stille](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stille#Middle_English "stille")*, *[styele](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=styele&action=edit&redlink=1 "styele (page does not exist)")*, *[style](#Middle_English)*, *[styill](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=styill&action=edit&redlink=1 "styill (page does not exist)")*, *[styll](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/styll#Middle_English "styll")*, *[styyl](https://en.wiktionary.org/w/index.php?title=styyl&action=edit&redlink=1 "styyl (page does not exist)")* (“writing tool, stylus; piece of written work; characteristic mode of expression, particularly one regarded as high quality; demeanour, manner, way of life; person's designation or title; stem of a plant; period of time”) (compare semantic development to *[по́черк](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D0%BF%D0%BE%D1%87%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%BA#Russian "почерк")* (póčerk, “handwriting, style”)),[^1] from [Old French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French "w:Old French") *[style](#Old_French)*, *[estile](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/estile#Old_French "estile")*, *[stil](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stil#Old_French "stil")*, *[stile](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stile#Old_French "stile")* (modern [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "w:French language") *[style](#French)*), or from [Medieval Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Latin "w:Medieval Latin") *[stylus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stylus#Latin "stylus")*, both from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[stilus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stilus#Latin "stilus")* (“pointed instrument, pale, spike, stake; writing tool, stylus; act of setting down in writing, composition; characteristic mode of expression, style; stem of a plant”), from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*(s)teyg-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/\(s\)teyg- "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/(s)teyg-")* (“to be sharp; to pierce, prick, puncture, stab; to goad”).[^2] [^3] [Doublet](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#doublet "Appendix:Glossary") of *[stylus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stylus#English "stylus")*.

The English word is cognate with [Catalan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalan_language "w:Catalan language") *[estil](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/estil#Catalan "estil")* (“engraving tool, stylus; gnomon; manner of doing something, style; fashionable skill, grace”), [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "w:German language") *[Stiel](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Stiel#German "Stiel")* (“handle; stalk”), [Italian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_language "w:Italian language") *[stilo](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stilo#Italian "stilo")* (“needle, stylus; fountain pen; beam; gnomon; part of pistil, style”), [Occitan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occitan_language "w:Occitan language") *[estil](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/estil#Occitan "estil")*, [Portuguese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language "w:Portuguese language") *[estilo](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/estilo#Portuguese "estilo")* (“writing tool, stylus; manner of doing something, style”), [Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language "w:Spanish language") *[estilo](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/estilo#Spanish "estilo")* (“writing tool, stylus; manner of doing something, style; fashionable skill, grace; part of pistil, style”).[^2]

The verb is derived from the noun.[^4]

**style** (*third-person singular simple present* **[styles](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/styles#English "styles")**, *present participle* **[styling](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/styling#English "styling")**, *simple past and past participle* **[styled](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/styled#English "styled")**)

1. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary")) To [design](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/design#English "design"), [fashion](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fashion#English "fashion"), [make](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/make#English "make"), or [arrange](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/arrange#English "arrange") in a certain way or form (style).
2. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), [formal](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#formal "Appendix:Glossary")) To [call](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/call#Verb "call") or [give](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/give#Verb "give") a [name](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/name#Noun "name") or [title](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/title#Noun "title") to. synonyms ▲ quotations ▼
	Synonyms: [designate](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/designate#English "designate"), [dub](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/dub#English "dub"), [name](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/name#English "name"); *see also* [Thesaurus:denominate](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Thesaurus:denominate#English "Thesaurus:denominate")
	*The pope is **styled** His or Your Holiness.*
3. ([transitive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#transitive "Appendix:Glossary"), [informal](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#informal "Appendix:Glossary")) To [create](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/create#English "create") for, or [give](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/give#Verb "give") to, someone a [style](#Noun), [fashion](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fashion#Noun "fashion"), or [image](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/image#Noun "image"), particularly one which is regarded as [attractive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/attractive#English "attractive"), [tasteful](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tasteful#English "tasteful"), or [trendy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trendy#English "trendy").
