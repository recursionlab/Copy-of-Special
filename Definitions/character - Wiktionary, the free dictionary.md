## English

### Etymology

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[caracter](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/caracter#Middle_English "caracter")*, from [Old French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French "w:Old French") *[caractere](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/caractere#Old_French "caractere")*, from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[character](#Latin)*, from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[χαρακτήρ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%87%CE%B1%CF%81%CE%B1%CE%BA%CF%84%CE%AE%CF%81#Ancient_Greek "χαρακτήρ")* (kharaktḗr, “type, nature, character”), from *[χαράσσω](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%87%CE%B1%CF%81%CE%AC%CF%83%CF%83%CF%89#Ancient_Greek "χαράσσω")* (kharássō, “to engrave”). [Doublet](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#doublet "Appendix:Glossary") of *[charakter](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/charakter#English "charakter")*.

#### Usage notes

- *Character* is sometimes used interchangeably with *reputation,* but the two words have different meanings; *character* describes the distinctive qualities of an individual or group while *reputation* describes the opinions held by others regarding an individual or group. *Character* is internal and authentic, while *reputation* is external and perceived.
