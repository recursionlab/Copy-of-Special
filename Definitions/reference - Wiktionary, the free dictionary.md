## English

### Etymology

From [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "w:Middle French") *[référence](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/r%C3%A9f%C3%A9rence#Middle_French "référence")*, from [Medieval Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Latin "w:Medieval Latin") *[referentia](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referentia#Latin "referentia")*, nominative neuter plural of *[referēns](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referens#Latin "referens")*, present participle of *[referō](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/refero#Latin "refero")* (“return, reply”, literally “carry back”). Morphologically + *[\-ence](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-ence#English "-ence")*.

### Noun

**reference** (*[countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary") and [uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")*, *plural* **[references](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/references#English "references")**)

1. ([literary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#literary "Appendix:Glossary") or [archaic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#archaic "Appendix:Glossary")) A [relationship](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/relationship#English "relationship") or [relation](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/relation#English "relation") (*[to](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/to#English "to")* something). quotations ▼
2. A [measurement](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/measurement#English "measurement") one can compare (some other measurement) to.
3. [Information](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/information#English "information") about a person, provided by someone (a [referee](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referee#English "referee")) with whom they are well acquainted. quotations ▼
4. (especially [US](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_English "w:American English")) A person who provides this information; a [referee](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referee#English "referee").
5. (often [attributive](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:English_nouns#Attributive "Appendix:English nouns")) A [reference work](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/reference_work#English "reference work").
	***reference** grammar* ― detailed linguistic description of a particular language's grammar
	***Reference** Dictionary of Linguistics*
6. The act of referring: a submitting for information or decision.
7. ([semantics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/semantics#English "semantics")) A [relation](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/relation#English "relation") between objects in which one object [designates](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/designate#English "designate"), or acts as a means by which to [connect](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/connect#English "connect") to or [link](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/link#English "link") to, another object.
8. (academic writing) A short written [identification](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/identification#English "identification") of a previously published work which is used as a [source](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/source#English "source") for a text.
9. (academic writing) A previously published [written](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/written#English "written") [work](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/work#English "work") thus indicated; a source.
10. ([computing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/computing#Noun "computing")) An object containing information which refers to data stored elsewhere, as opposed to containing the data itself.
11. ([programming](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming#Noun "programming"), character entity) A special sequence used to represent complex characters in markup languages, such as `&trade;` for the ™ symbol.
12. ([obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary")) [Appeal](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/appeal#English "appeal"). quotations ▼

### Verb

**reference** (*third-person singular simple present* **[references](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/references#English "references")**, *present participle* **[referencing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referencing#English "referencing")**, *simple past and past participle* **[referenced](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/referenced#English "referenced")**)

1. To provide a list of references for (a text).
	*You must thoroughly **reference** your paper before submitting it.*
2. (occasionally [proscribed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#proscribed "Appendix:Glossary")) To [cite](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cite#English "cite"), to use as a reference. quotations ▼
	***Reference** the dictionary for word meanings.*
3. (sometimes [proscribed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#proscribed "Appendix:Glossary")) To [mention](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mention#English "mention"), to [refer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/refer#English "refer") to. quotations ▼
	*In his speech, the candidate obliquely **referenced** the past failures of his opponent.*
4. ([programming](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming#Noun "programming")) To contain the [value](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/value#English "value") that is a [memory](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/memory#English "memory") [address](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/address#English "address") of some value stored in memory.
	*The given pointer will **reference** the actual generated data.*

#### Usage notes

- Some authorities object to the use of *reference* as a verb with a meaning other than “provide a list of references for,” preferring or *[cite](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cite#English "cite")* in these cases. Others allow the meaning “cite \[as evidence, an example, etc.\]” but reject “mention.” [^2] Nevertheless, the proscribed usages are common in both writing and speech.
