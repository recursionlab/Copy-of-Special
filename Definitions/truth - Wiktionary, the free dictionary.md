### Etymology

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[trouthe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trouthe#Middle_English "trouthe")*, *[truthe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/truthe#Middle_English "truthe")*, *[trewthe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trewthe#Middle_English "trewthe")*, *[treowthe](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/treowthe#Middle_English "treowthe")*, from [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[trēowþ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/treow%C3%BE#Old_English "treowþ")*, *[trīewþ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/triew%C3%BE#Old_English "triewþ")* (“truth, veracity, faith, fidelity, loyalty, honour, pledge, covenant”), from [Proto-Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Germanic_language "w:Proto-Germanic language") *[\*triwwiþō](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/triwwi%C3%BE%C5%8D "Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/triwwiþō")* (“promise, covenant, contract”), from [Proto-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "w:Proto-Indo-European language") *[\*drew-](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/drew- "Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/drew-")* (“firm, solid”), equivalent to *[true](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/true#English "true")* + *[\-th](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-th#English:_abstract_nominal "-th")* (abstract nominal suffix). [Doublet](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#doublet "Appendix:Glossary") of *[troth](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/troth#English "troth")*. Cognate with [Norwegian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norwegian_language "w:Norwegian language") *[trygd](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trygd#Norwegian "trygd")* (“trustworthiness, security, insurance”), [Icelandic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icelandic_language "w:Icelandic language") *[tryggð](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/trygg%C3%B0#Icelandic "tryggð")* (“loyalty, fidelity”).

### Noun

**truth** (*usually [uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")*, *plural* **[truths](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/truths#English "truths")**)

1. True facts, genuine depiction or statements of reality. quotations ▼
	*The **truth** is that our leaders knew a lot more than they were letting on.*
2. Conformity to [fact](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fact#English "fact") or [reality](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/reality#English "reality"); [correctness](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/correctness#English "correctness"), [accuracy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/accuracy#English "accuracy"). quotations ▼
	*There was some **truth** in his statement that he had no other choice.*
3. The state or quality of being [true](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/true#English "true") to someone or something.
	***Truth** to one's own feelings is all-important in life.*
4. ([archaic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#archaic "Appendix:Glossary")) [Faithfulness](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/faithfulness#English "faithfulness"), [fidelity](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fidelity#English "fidelity"). quotations ▼
5. ([obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary")) A pledge of loyalty or faith.
6. Conformity to rule; exactness; close correspondence with an example, mood, model, etc. quotations ▼
7. That which is [real](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/real#English "real"), in a deeper sense; spiritual or ‘genuine’ reality. quotations ▼
	*The **truth** is what is.*
	*Alcoholism and redemption led me finally to **truth**.*
8. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary")) Something acknowledged to be true; a true statement or axiom. quotations ▼
	*Hunger and jealousy are just eternal **truths** of human existence.*
9. ([physics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/physics#English "physics"), [dated](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#dated "Appendix:Glossary")) [Topness](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/topness#English "topness"); the property of a [truth quark](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/truth_quark#English "truth quark").
10. ([games](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/game#Noun "game")) In the game [truth or dare](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/truth_or_dare#English "truth or dare"), the choice to truthfully answer a question put forth.
	*When asked truth or dare, he picked **truth**.*
