## English

### Etymology

From [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "w:Middle French") *[métaphore](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/m%C3%A9taphore#Middle_French "métaphore")*, from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[metaphora](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/metaphora#Latin "metaphora")*, from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[μεταφορά](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BC%CE%B5%CF%84%CE%B1%CF%86%CE%BF%CF%81%CE%AC#Ancient_Greek "μεταφορά")* (metaphorá), from *[μεταφέρω](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BC%CE%B5%CF%84%CE%B1%CF%86%CE%AD%CF%81%CF%89#Ancient_Greek "μεταφέρω")* (metaphérō, “to transfer, apply”), from *[μετά](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BC%CE%B5%CF%84%CE%AC#Ancient_Greek "μετά")* (metá, “with, across, after”) + *[φέρω](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%86%CE%AD%CF%81%CF%89#Ancient_Greek "φέρω")* (phérō, “to bear, carry”).

### Noun

**metaphor** (*[countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary") and [uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary")*, *plural* **[metaphors](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/metaphors#English "metaphors")**)

1. ([uncountable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#uncountable "Appendix:Glossary"), [rhetoric](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rhetoric#English "rhetoric")) The use of a word, phrase, concept, or set of concepts to refer to something other than its literal meaning, invoking an implicit similarity between the thing described and what is denoted by the word, etc., that is used. coordinate term ▲ quotations ▼
	Coordinate term: [simile](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/simile#English "simile") (when the similarity is made explicit by the words *like* or *as*)
2. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary"), [rhetoric](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rhetoric#English "rhetoric")) A word or phrase used in such implied comparison. quotations ▼
3. ([countable](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#countable "Appendix:Glossary"), [graphical user interface](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/graphical_user_interface#English "graphical user interface")) The use of an [everyday](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/everyday#English "everyday") [object](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/object#English "object") or [concept](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/concept#English "concept") to [represent](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/represent#English "represent") an underlying facet of the [computer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/computer#English "computer") and thus aid [users](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/user#English "user") in performing [tasks](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/task#English "task").
