### Etymology

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[primitif](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitif#Middle_English "primitif")*, from [Old French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_French "w:Old French") *[primitif](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitif#Old_French "primitif")*, from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[prīmitīvus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitivus#Latin "primitivus")* (“first or earliest of its kind”), from *[prīmus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primus#Latin "primus")* (“first”); see [prime](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prime "prime"). [Doublet](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#doublet "Appendix:Glossary") of *[primitivo](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitivo#English "primitivo")*.

### Noun

**primitive** (*plural* **[primitives](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primitives#English "primitives")**)

1. ([linguistics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/linguistics#English "linguistics")) An original or primary word; a word not derived from another, as opposed to *[derivative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/derivative#English "derivative")*.
2. A [member](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/member#English "member") of a primitive [society](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/society#English "society").
3. Primitive or [primeval](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/primeval#English "primeval") nature; the [innate](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/innate#English "innate"), instinctive [element](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/element#English "element") within a person; the deep, instinctive, [precultural](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/precultural#English "precultural") layer of human nature. quotations ▼
4. Natural or [premodern](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/premodern#English "premodern") environment or conditions; life lacking modern [technology](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/technology#English "technology") and society. quotations ▼
5. A simple-minded [person](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/person#English "person").
6. ([programming](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming#Noun "programming")) A [data type](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/data_type#English "data type") that is built into the [programming language](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming_language#English "programming language"), as opposed to more complex structures.
7. ([programming](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming#Noun "programming")) Any of the simplest elements (instructions, statements, etc.) available in a [programming language](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/programming_language#English "programming language").[^1] quotations ▼
8. A basic [geometric](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/geometric#English "geometric") shape from which more complex shapes can be constructed.
9. ([mathematics](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mathematics#English "mathematics")) A function whose [derivative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/derivative#English "derivative") is a given function; an [antiderivative](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/antiderivative#English "antiderivative").
