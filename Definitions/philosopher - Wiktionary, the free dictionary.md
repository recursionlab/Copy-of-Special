## English

### Alternative forms

- [phylosopher](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/phylosopher#English "phylosopher") ([obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary"))

### Etymology

From [Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "w:Middle English") *[philosophre](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philosophre#Middle_English "philosophre")*, from [Anglo-Norman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Norman_language "w:Anglo-Norman language") or [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "w:Middle French") , from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "w:Latin") *[philosophus](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philosophus#Latin "philosophus")*, from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "w:Ancient Greek") *[φιλόσοφος](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%86%CE%B9%CE%BB%CF%8C%CF%83%CE%BF%CF%86%CE%BF%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "φιλόσοφος")* (philósophos, literally “ [lover](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/lover "lover") of [wisdom](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wisdom "wisdom") ”) + *[\-er](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-er#English "-er")*.

Credited as having been coined by [Pythagoras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pythagoras "w:Pythagoras") to describe himself.[^1] [^2]

Displaced native [Old English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_English "w:Old English") *[ūþwita](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/u%C3%BEwita#Old_English "uþwita")*.

### Noun

**philosopher** (*plural* **[philosophers](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philosophers#English "philosophers")**)

1. ([originally](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/originally#English "originally")) A lover of wisdom. antonym ▲
	Antonym: [misosopher](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/misosopher#English "misosopher")
2. A student of [philosophy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/philosophy#English "philosophy").
3. A scholar or expert engaged in or contributing to philosophical inquiry. hyponym ▼ quotations ▼
4. ([archaic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#archaic "Appendix:Glossary")) A person who applies the principles of philosophy to the conduct of their life, as by acting calmly and rationally in the face of inevitable change. quotations ▼
5. ([archaic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#archaic "Appendix:Glossary")) A student, scholar, or expert in any branch of knowledge, especially those branches studied prior to being considered part of pure [science](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/science#English "science").
6. ([obsolete](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#obsolete "Appendix:Glossary")) An [alchemist](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/alchemist#English "alchemist"). quotations ▼
