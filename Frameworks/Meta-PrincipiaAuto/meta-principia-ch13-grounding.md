# METAPRINCIPIA AUTOPOIĒTICA — Chapter 13: Grounding (SUMMARY)

**Layer:** A — Canon (summarized)
**Summarized:** 2026-05-21

## Core Problem

How do symbols acquire meaning? How does a symbol s come to be about something outside the formal system?

## The Three Classical Answers (and why they fail)

1. **Formalism**: Symbols have no meaning. Math is symbol manipulation. (Fails: why does math work?)
2. **Platonism**: Symbols refer to abstract objects. (Fails: how do we access abstract objects?)
3. **Intuitionism**: Symbols refer to mental constructions. (Fails: how do we share mental constructions?)

## The Autopoietic Answer

Symbols are grounded in the **regen loop**. A symbol is grounded when it participates in a cycle:
```
World → U → s → R → Action → World' → U → s' → R → ...
```
Where U = perception, R = regeneration. The cycle is closed by the agent's action on the world.

## Key Definition

**Grounding Cycle**: A closed loop where:
1. s is produced by perceiving the world (U)
2. s is consumed to produce action (R)
3. Action changes the world
4. The changed world produces new symbols

A symbol is grounded when it participates in this cycle. Ungrounded symbols are "floating" — they have syntax but no semantics.

## Temple Interpretation

The temple's chambers are grounded when they connect to other chambers (cross-references) AND to the world (sparring, research, external validation). An ungrounded chamber is an orphan — it has content but no connections. The grounding cycle IS the cross-reference cycle.
