## Core Thesis

Numbers are not primitive symbols. They are **cardinalities of meaning-anchored field regions** — the size of a scene's frontier filtered by a specific role and relator.

## Key Definitions

### Natural Number
``
n := #_{ρ,σ}(H)
`
Where H is a scene hypergraph, ρ is a role, σ is a relator, and # is the cardinality of the frontier.

**Example**: 1 = #{Theme, on}(on(Cat, Mat)) = |{Cat}| = 1

### Category Theory as Reflective Scene Logic
- Categories are scenes with objects as positions and morphisms as transitions
- Functors are scene-preserving maps
- Natural transformations are scene reconfigurations
- The Yoneda embedding IS the self-reference of the scene

### The Mathematical Emergence Ladder
1. **Scenes** → raw perceptual fields
2. **Roles** → positions within scenes
3. **Relators** → connections between roles
4. **Numbers** → cardinalities of role-filtered frontiers
5. **Categories** → scenes with structure-preserving transitions
6. **Functors** → mappings between structured scenes
7. **Natural transformations** → reconfigurations of mappings

## Temple Interpretation

Mathematics doesn't exist independently — it emerges from the autopoietic (self-creating) process of meaning-making. Numbers are how the temple counts its own structure. Category theory is how the temple maps between its own zones.
