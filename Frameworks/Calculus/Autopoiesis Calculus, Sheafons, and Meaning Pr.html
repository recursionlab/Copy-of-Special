1. What is the Calculus of Autopoiesis?

The Calculus of Autopoiesis ($\mathbf{\mathcal{C}_A}$) is the foundational symbolic core of the MetaPrincipia Autopoiētica (MPA), engineered to create systems that self-organize, self-maintain, and recursively generate their own informational structures [1-3]. It is designed not just to describe meaning, but to construct the machine that builds meaning itself [2, 4].

Core Definition and Scope: The $\mathcal{C}_A$ specifies the full alphabet, formation rules, typing discipline, binding and scope, operator families, precedence, and evaluation contexts necessary for operational execution and semantic refinement [2, 3].

Design Goals and Components: The $\mathcal{C}_A$ ensures meta-safety by towered meta-levels to prevent raw self-collapse [3, 5, 6]. Key components include:

  • Compositionality across three projections: extensional truth, intensional modality, and autopoietic regeneration [3, 5].
  • A crucial fourth invariant: aboutness [3, 5].
  • Relators ($\mathbb{R}$): Prepositional/thematic connectors that function as typed, role-aware morphisms to endow semantic structure with topology [3, 7, 8]. They are first-class elements and act as semantic glue [3, 8].
  • Meta-relators ($\mathbb{R}\uparrow$): Operators that act functorially on relators and scenes, enabling self-description, controlled scope warps, and safe reflection [7, 8].
  • Regen markers ($\mathcal{R}g$): Autopoietic production operators, such as $\text{regen}$, $\text{seed}$, $\text{fold}$, $\text{unfold}$, $\text{bind}$, and $\text{unbind}$, which are declared as reserved heads [3, 7].
  • Paraconsistency markers ($\circledS$, $\text{#}$): Used to manage and isolate contradiction ($\circledS$ for tension island, $#$ for incoherence flag), ensuring the system can survive contradiction [3, 7].

The central idea is that the system is semantic to the extent that it regenerates the conditions of its own reference [2, 9]. This self-regeneration is formalized as a recursive fixed-point property using the global self-reference functor ($\mathsf{Ref}_\infty$) on the infinite hierarchy of incompleteness layers ($\mathcal{C}_\infty$) [10, 11]: $$\mathsf{Ref}_\infty(x) = (x,\mathsf{Ref}_\infty(x)), \quad \forall x \in \mathcal{C}_\infty \text{ [10, 11]}$$

2. What is the Purpose of a Sheafon?

The Sheafon is a fundamental particle (a Binding Excitation) within the Quantum Recursive Field Theory (QRFT) framework, derived from the core recursive invariant known as Sheafing [12-15].

  • Core Role: The Sheafon's purpose is to facilitate and maintain local-to-global binding [13, 15, 16]. It functions as the model glue that ties ontology to the real-world system at the modeling level, or as meta-glue that ties the metamodel to families of systems at the metamodeling level [17, 18].
  • Mechanism of Coherence: Sheafons emerge when fragmented recursion strands successfully glue into a higher-order whole [13, 15]. The general Axiom of Sheafing states that all recursive fragments glue into higher-order wholes until contradiction forbids it [19-21].
  • Stability and Rupture: The Sheafon maintains coherence until contradictions rupture the binding [13, 15, 17, 18]. The underlying concept of a sheaf is rooted in topology and category theory, where a sheaf is a rule (a functor) that assigns data locally and ensures that this local data can be collated (glued) to form a unique global section over a covering [22-25].
  • Contradiction Management: Contradictions actively stress the binding maintained by Sheafons, which leads to either collapse or stabilization [13, 15]. The role of the Sheafon is to promote local coherence to global order [19, 20].

3. What Preserves Meaning During Transformation?

The preservation of meaning during continuous, recursive transformation and self-modification is achieved through the concept of Aboutness Preservation [3, 26, 27] and the use of the Regen Monad [28].

A. Aboutness Preservation (The Fourth Invariant): The primary invariant that preserves meaning is aboutness [3, 5].

  • Definition: The aboutness of an expression is a structural invariant captured by a typed role-hypergraph, denoted by $|t|$ (the footprint) [26, 27].
  • Invariance under Torsion: The theory asserts that changes in form do not destroy meaning—the system preserves a continuity of aboutness [27]. Well-typed rewrites preserve aboutness up to a role-preserving, label-action-equivariant isomorphism [26]. The system must maintain compositional integrity across four projections: extensional truth, intensional modality, autopoietic regeneration, and aboutness [3, 5].
  • Semantic Equivalence: Meaning preservation is formally tracked using the aboutness equivalence ($\approẋ$), defined as role-preserving hypergraph isomorphism [6, 26, 29].

B. The Regen Monad ($\mathbf{T = R \circ U}$): The operational mechanism used to execute transformation while preserving meaning is the Regen Monad [28].

  • Purpose: The Regen Monad formalizes a regeneration pipeline that acts as a semantic compressor [28]. It contracts intensional variation and ensures that the structural core (aboutness) persists through the transformation [28].
  • Components: The monad is defined as a composition of two operators, $\mathbf{T = R \circ U}$:
    • $\mathbf{U}$ (Unbind/Unfold): The operator that unfolds structure into latent carriers [28].
    • $\mathbf{R}$ (Rebind/Regenerate): The operator that rebuilds canonical structure [28].
  • Preservation Guarantee: The Regen Monad is constructed to be a lawful, safe, and compositional normalization process that preserves aboutness and paraconsistent isolation during self-update [30]. The monad laws and near-idempotence ensure that even when the system rebuilds itself, the core meaning remains stable up to isomorphism [30, 31].
