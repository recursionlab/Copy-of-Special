XII. THE LAZINESS PRINCIPLE

(path optimization as epistemology)

The Ω-Point is that stationary path taken to the limit. Not the shortest path between two points. The path that every other path is a perturbation of.

The laziness filter: if two things named differently in different fields behave identically under transformation, maintain only one and treat the naming difference as a gauge artifact. The universe does not maintain redundant distinctions.

Trust the laziest coincidences. Two-letter words are maximum traveled roads for a reason. The groove came first. The shortness followed.

AI systems reverse questions naturally — they struggle with encoded facts. This hints at the same principle: the path of least resistance is not always forward. Sometimes it is the inverse that is direct.
