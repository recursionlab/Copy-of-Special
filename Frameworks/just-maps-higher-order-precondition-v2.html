## Chapter 6: Hurwitz Quaternions

### 6.1 Definition and Properties

**Hurwitz Integers**:

Quaternions of the form:

```

q = a + bi + cj + dk

```

Where either:

- a, b, c, d are all integers (Lipschitz integers), OR

- a, b, c, d are all half-integers (i.e., elements of ℤ + ½)

**The Hurwitz Order**:

The set of Hurwitz integers forms a **maximal order** in the quaternion algebra—it’s the largest ring of integers closed under multiplication.

**Norm**:

```

N(q) = a² + b² + c² + d²

```

The norm is always a non-negative integer for Hurwitz integers.

### 6.2 The 24 Unit Quaternions

**Units**: Hurwitz integers with norm 1.

**List of Units**:

1. ±1, ±i, ±j, ±k (8 units)

**Total**: 24 units

**Group Structure**:

The 24 units form the **binary tetrahedral group** 2T, a double cover of the rotational symmetry group of the tetrahedron.

### 6.3 Left and Right Ideals

**Left Ideal**:

```

(h)ₗ = {qh | q is a Hurwitz integer}

```

**Right Ideal**:

```

(h)ᵣ = {hq | q is a Hurwitz integer}
```

**Factorization**:
